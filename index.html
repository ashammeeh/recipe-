Traditional Stuffed Grape Leaves

Stuffed grape leaves is a traditional dish that originates from the Middle East. Referred to as “warak dawali” in Arabic, the dish calls for grape leaves stuffed with spiced rice and finely ground beef. The leaves are then to be cooked in a pot with traditionally, tomatoes, and stuffed zucchinis at the bottom, and to be flipped when served.

Ingredients

Grape Leaf Filling

  • 1 cup uncooked, short-grain rice
  • 2 large onions, chopped finely
  • 5 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 pounds ground beef
  • juice of 2 1/2 lemons
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon nutmeg
  • 1/2 teaspoon alspice
  • two cloves of garlic, pounded

    • Grape Leaves

    8 cups water
  • 1/2 cup lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 pound grape leaves

    • Instructions

  • Rinse leaves then place in boiling water for 3-5 minutes - until softened.
  • Heat 1/2 cup olive oil in a skillet, sauté onions for about 5 minutes, then stir in lemon juice.
  • Line a saucepan with thin slices of tomato, stuffed zucchini -by carving them out first, then stuffing it with about two teaspoons -, and flat grape leaves.
  • Stuff the grape leaves with stuffing by placing about a teaspoon of stuffing in the center, and folding over the sides then top, then rolling in your direction.
  • Place stuffed grape leaves on top of stuffed zucchini, then pour remaining 1/2 cup olive oil and water to cover the layers over by an inch.
  • Cover pan and leave to boil, then, lower heat to simmer for 45-60 minutes - until the leaves are tender and rice is cooked.
